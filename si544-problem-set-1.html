Based upon the tibble and the boxplot, there does not seem to be a large relationship between rating and number of players. By observing the boxplot, one could claim that that as player count increases, the spread decreases. However, that is a mistaken inference because as maximum number of players increases, so does the number of games with that many maximum players. To prove this, I created a histogram visualizing the number of games by maximum players. It is clear that there are far fewer games with a larger number of maximum players.

What set of maximum player counts appear to have higher ratings?

Returning to the tibble, we can see that three player games have the highest mean rating. I created a visualization of mean ratings using a scatterplot to make this clearer. It is possible that those 5 games in particular are awesome games and as a result rate higher than other games. In fact, I would argue that 2 and 4 player games maximum player count is more associated to their higher rating than 3 person games.
