The Data

The data set for this assignment consists of college admissions data. It comes from an open data set available via https://www.kaggle.com/mohansacharya/graduate-admissions .

The dataset contains several parameters which are considered important during the application for Masters Programs. The parameters included are:

  1. GRE Scores ( out of 340 )
  2. TOEFL Scores ( out of 120 )
  3. University Rating ( out of 5 )
  4. Statement of Purpose and Letter of Recommendation Strength ( out of 5 )
  5. Undergraduate GPA ( out of 10 )
  6. Research Experience ( either 0 or 1 )
  7. Chance of Admit ( ranging from 0 to 1 )

The full citation for the data is:

Mohan S Acharya, Asfia Armaan, Aneeta S Antony : A Comparison of Regression Models for Prediction of Graduate Admissions, IEEE International Conference on Computational Intelligence in Data Science 2019.

Q6 Response:

To create a regression model observing the relationship between chance of admission with cumulative GPA and research experience as explanatory variables, I used the lm() function. Instead of assuming there are parallel slopes, I am assuming there is an interaction between the two. As such, Ive included the term cgpa:research in the function for the linear model, which will compute the model as if there is an interaction. Viewing the model with get_regression_table, we get these two equations:

For no research experience:

admit = -0.834 + 0.178 * gpa

For research exp:

admit = -0.834 + (-0.152) + (0.178 + 0.022) * gpa

To calculate the estimtaed chance of admission for a student with research experience and a cumulative GPA of 9.0, we plug the values into the second equation.

admit = -0.834 + (-0.152) + (0.178 + 0.022) * 9
admit = 0.814

The estimated chance of admission is 0.814 or 81.4% for a student with research experience and a cumulative GPA of 9.0.

Q8 Response:

To determine whether letter of recommendation or statement of purpose strength is more important for admission chance, I created several visualizations and performed linear regressions for both variables as they relate to admissions chance. Initially, I created scatterplots to visualize the relationship. However, as one can see above, the scatterplots all cluster on 0.5 increments due to the nature of the data. A jittered scatterplot misrepresents the data as well. In order to visually represent the data, I created boxplots that are factored upon statement of purpose and letter of recommendation scores. Observing the boxplots, we can see in both plots that as statement of purpose and letter of recommendation scores increase, so does the chance of admission. The differences between these two boxplots are relatively small. Both sets of boxplots have long whiskers indicating a large amount of variance within the admission chance ranges for those scores. Lets look at the regression models to see if there is a significant difference there.

Looking at the regression models from the get_regression_table() function, we get these two equations:

For statement of purpose:

chance_of_admission_hat = 0.399 + 0.096 * statement_of_purpose_score

For letter of recommendation:

letter_of_recommendation_hat = 0.357 + 0.106 * letter_of_recommendation_score

Given these equations, it is difficult to tell whether statement of purpose score or letter of recommendation score is more important for admission chance. On the one hand, letter of recommendation score has a higher slope, meaning that each point of score towards their letter of recommendation is associated with a higher gain in admission chance compared to an equal increase in statement of purpose score. However, statement of purpose has a higher intercept, meaning that statement of purpose scores start higher to begin with compared to letter of recommendation scores. There is no clear winner in terms of which is more important for increasing admission chance. This is especially so if you look at the confidence intervals for both regressions. The confidence intervals overlap for both the intercept and the slope for each regression. This means that there is a possibility that both letter of recommendation and statement of purpose have the same slope and intercept and that the variation we see is only a result of variation in sampling. This probably isnt the case, but it is not impossible to imagine given the current confidence intervals. As such, there does not seem to be a clear winner in terms of letter of recommendation score and statement of purpose score on chance of admission.
